Announcements

Emails about Code, Extra Credit Requests, Quizzes

Emails about Code:
If you send me an email about issues with code, please include an attachment of your zipped lab folder. I will leave comments, rezip it, and send it back to you as soon as possible. Remember to put ENS1300: Lab## in the subject line.

Extra Credit Opportunities:
Several of you have already taken advantage of the first extra credit opportunities. In general, these are the steps for getting your points:

  1. Send me an email
    • Corrections
      • Subject line example: ENS1300: lab04.pdf Correction
      • Subject line example: ENS1300: Lab 4 Website Correction
      • Describe the mistake and general location in the email
    • Contributions
      • Subject line example: ENS1300: MATLAB Example
      • Subject line example: ENS1300: C++ Example
      • Attach a zip file that includes all required files for your proposal
      • If you're proposing a MATLAB example, only that code is required
      • If you're proposing an example in another language, an equivalent MATLAB verision is also required
      • Your code must be adequately documented/commented
  2. Await confirmation
    • I will reply with an approval/rejection email as soon as possible
  3. Document your points
    • After you receive confirmation from me, create an EXTRAPOINTS.txt (or EXTRAPOINTS.m) file in the current lab folder you're working in
    • Include a brief description of the reason for your extra credit and the number of points awarded
      • There should be a line in this file for every correction/example approved that week
    • Be sure to include the EXTRAPOINTS file in your next lab zip file submission. This will remind me to officially award your points while I'm grading.

Quizzes:
I think it will be better to use our lecture time for going through examples and working on the lab assignments rather than taking in-class quizzes. From now on, quizzes will open and close at the same time as their respective lab assignments. You'll have 20 minutes to complete each quiz, but you can take it at any time while it's open.

17 February 2020

Lab Due Dates, Extra Credit Opportunities

Lab Due Dates:
Lab submissions will now be due on Mondays at the beginning of class (8:10 for P01, 9:40 for P03). I will accept late submissions via email, but a 10-point penalty will be applied for each 24 hour period after the due date. For example:
● Submit on a Monday afternoon   →   max grade of 90
● Submit on a Tuesday afternoon   →   max grade of 80
● Submit on a Wednesday afternoon   →   max grade of 70

Extra Credit Opportunities:
I will award extra credit for each mistake you find in the website material or lab assignment pdf.

Mistake Class Examples Point Value
important misspelled or missing words, significant grammatical errors, incorrect/missing due dates, etc 0.5
major broken links, inconsistent deliverable/variable names, rubric errors, etc 1
critical mistakes in code examples 2

I will also award extra credit to anyone who would like to contribute to the course materials by writing equivalent examples in other languages or by making significant improvements to my MATLAB examples. Any accepted contributions will be credited to you. The number of extra points awarded will be decided on a case-by-case basis, so you may want to talk to me before spending any time developing your idea. For example, this would not qualify:

MATLAB

x = 5.678;

C++

double x = 5.678;
